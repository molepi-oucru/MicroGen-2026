Day 4 introduces Next-generation Metagenomic sequencing (mNGS) as a
practical tool for surveillance, diagnosis, and clinical decision-making
in clinical microbiology. The lessons use compact teaching re-analyses
so that you can focus on the workflow and analysis results
interpretation.

In \href{vietnamese-healthy-gut-microbiome.qmd}{mNGS in clinical
microbiology}, you will work with a six-sample demonstration re-analysis
adapted from Pereira-Dias et al.~The exercise uses a subset of a
Vietnamese healthy gut microbiome and antimicrobial resistance
surveillance dataset to show how shotgun metagenomic reads can be
processed into taxonomic and AMR summaries. This is a teaching
demonstration, not a full reproduction of the original paper.

In \href{clinical-reasoning.qmd}{mNGS in pathogen detection}, you will
move from surveillance to a clinical metagenomics case. The lesson
focuses on when unbiased sequencing is useful, how infection results
should be interpreted, and how metagenomic evidence supports clinical
reasoning rather than replacing it.
